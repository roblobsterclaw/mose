Competitive Moat — Four Pillars Analysis

💰 Cost Advantages
Amazon's logistics network (1,000+ warehouses, owned freight, last-mile delivery) creates structural cost advantages. AWS benefits from massive scale economics — the more customers, the lower marginal cost per unit of compute. Third-party sellers subsidize Amazon's fixed cost base, creating a self-reinforcing flywheel. No legacy retailer can replicate 25 years of logistics infrastructure investment.
🔒 Switching Costs
AWS: migrating enterprise cloud infrastructure is massively expensive (years of rearchitecting, retraining, re-integrating). Enterprises that build on Amazon SageMaker, Bedrock, Lambda, etc. are deeply embedded. Prime membership (200M+ subscribers) creates behavioral lock-in. Marketplace sellers depend on Amazon for 50-80% of their revenue — they can't leave even when they want to.
🌐 Network Effects
Classic two-sided marketplace: more buyers attract more sellers, more sellers improve selection/price, which attracts more buyers. AWS has developer network effects — engineers trained on AWS create jobs requiring AWS knowledge, driving enterprise adoption. Advertising gets stronger as more purchase data accumulates. Amazon's retail data (what people search, browse, buy) is unmatched.
🧠 Intangible Assets
Amazon brand = trust + speed + everything-store. AWS brand = infrastructure reliability (99.999% uptime SLAs). Prime brand = value. Alexa ecosystem. 25+ years of customer behavior data. Regulatory licenses and certifications across AWS (government cloud, healthcare). Patented fulfillment innovations. Jeff Bezos's "regret minimization" institutional culture of invention.
🏰 MOAT RATING: WIDEST Amazon has one of the three or four widest moats in global capitalism. AWS + Advertising + Logistics + Marketplace = four interlocking moats. Impossible to fully replicate at any price.
