Why This Is Mispriced

Complexity Discount
Brookfield is a holding company, asset manager, insurer, real asset owner, and public-market sponsor at the same time. That structure makes it hard for screens to value, especially when IFRS net income is noisy.
Cashflow Better Than EPS
Company-reported distributable earnings before realizations reached $5.4B, or $2.27 per share, in 2025. Total distributable earnings were $6.3B.
Embedded Carry
Brookfield completed roughly $91B of monetizations in 2025 and expects asset sale activity to convert more carried interest into income over the next several years.
AI Infrastructure Tailwind
Brookfield is positioned around power, data centers, grid capacity, renewables, and nuclear. It is selling picks and shovels to AI demand, not betting on a single model winner.

Key Risks

  • Accounting opacity: Distributable earnings are non-GAAP/non-IFRS and require trust in management's bridge from accounting income to owner cashflow.
  • Real estate marks: Office and certain property exposures can drag confidence, even if the broader platform is diversified.
  • Leverage and rates: Brookfield is built around financing assets. Higher-for-longer rates reduce exit multiples and slow monetizations.
  • Insurance liabilities: Wealth solutions adds permanent capital but also adds duration, spread, and regulatory risk.
  • Complexity discount may persist: The stock can remain cheap for a long time because the story is difficult to underwrite quickly.
  • Manager key-person risk: Bruce Flatt and senior leadership are central to the culture, even with deep succession planning.

Monitoring Checklist

  • Distributable earnings before realizations per share: should continue compounding at a double-digit rate.
  • Fee-related earnings growth in asset management: confirms fundraising quality, not just one-time asset sales.
  • Wealth solutions spread income and capital deployment: watch whether growth is profitable and conservatively matched.
  • Real estate valuation disclosures: look for transparency, realized exits, and reduced office concern.
  • Share repurchases: buybacks below intrinsic value are a strong signal from management.
  • AI infrastructure pipeline: power, data center, nuclear, and grid deals should convert into funded opportunities, not just headlines.
